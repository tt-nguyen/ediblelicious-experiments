Starters Thai Crab Cakes

For the Crab Cakes

  • 75g French beans, finely chopped
  • 200g fresh white crabmeat
  • 1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 4 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • 1 lime, zest
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander
  • 75g fresh white breadcrumbs
  • 1 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Sunflower oil, for frying
  1. Cook the beans in boiling salted water for 1-2 minutes until tender. Drain and refresh them under cold running water, then leave to dry on kitchen paper.
  2. Mix the crab meat with the cooked beans and the remaining ingredients (apart from the sunflower oil), and season the mixture with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  3. Shape the mixture into 8 small patties.
  4. Heat 1-2 tbsp sunflower oil in a frying pan and cook 4 crab cakes at a time until golden brown on both sides. Remove from the pan and keep warm while you cook the remainder.
  5. Serve with the sweet chilli dipping sauce.

For the Dipping Sauce

  • 5 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 2 red chillies, roughly chopped
  • 6cm piece ginger, roughly chopped
  • 2 dried kaffir lime leaves
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander
  • 175g caster sugar
  • 50ml rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (or lime juice)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  1. Blend the garlic, chillies, ginger, lime leaves, lemongrass and coriander in a food processor until very finely chopped.
  2. Tip the sugar into a small saucepan, add 3 tbsp water and heat gently until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Continue to cook for another min or so until the syrup starts to thicken slightly.
  4. Add the chilli and ginger mixture, and continue to cook for a further 2-3 minutes.
  5. Then stir in the rice vinegar, fish sauce and soy sauce.
  6. Remove from the heat and leave to cool to room temperature.
